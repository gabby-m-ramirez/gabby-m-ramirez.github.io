Biography

Discovering my Direction

My academic journey began at the College of William & Mary, where I purused a B.A. in Psychology with the initial goal of becoming a counselor. I later pursued a M.A. in Clinical Mental Health Counseling at Regent University, but after a year I realized the field was not the right fit for me. Upon exiting graduate school, I started working in the automotive industry, where I quickly advanced from receptionist to warranty administrator and eventually assistant to the service director. In this role, I served as a link between the manufacturer and the dealership, gaining experience in warranty operations, employee management, and accounting over seven years.

A New Academic Chapter

Eventually, I decided I wanted something else for my career and decided to pursue a new direction. Remembering my interest of programming, I returned to school at Old Dominion University to study Computer Science while continuing to work full time. I completed my B.S. in Computer Science in December 2024 and began my career as a Computer Scientist with Naval Information Warfare Center Atlantic (NIWC LANT).

Current Role

As a Computer Scientist and a project lead at NIWC LANT, I am leading a pilot project while pursuing my M.S. in Computer Science at Old Dominion University. In my role, I contribute to both frontend and backend development, working with tools such as Qlik, Databricks, Python, and PySpark. I also support and lead Agile processes with requirements gathering and Jira management.
